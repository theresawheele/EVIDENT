Designing future Emergency Departments with evidence and extended reality testing.

EVIDENT (SNF funded, 2025–2028) is an interdisciplinary project led by researchers from ETH Zurich, University of Cambridge, Northumbria and clinical & architecture partners. We will run an international architectural competition and test selected designs in an extended reality simulation at the Swiss Centre for Design and Health.

Competition details Meet the team

What we study

Our central hypothesis is that visibility within the Emergency Department strongly influence the communication and collaboration between Healthcare Workers, resulting in an improved quality of care for patients. We will evaluate how intelligible layouts facilitate communication and coordination, and compare them with spatially dysfunctional (unintelligible) layouts that disrupt those processes.

International competition

Two parallel competitions: one for professionals (architects) and one for students. Winning designs will be prototyped in XR.

XR testing

Extended reality simulation at the Swiss Centre for Design and Health to test spatial behaviour and interaction.

Empirical evaluation

We will use tracking technologies, wearables, and video capture to measure interaction patterns with hired participants.

Study design snapshot

  • Participants: ~20 healthcare workers + ~80 actors (patients & relatives)
  • Measurements: location tracking, wearable sensors, video capture
