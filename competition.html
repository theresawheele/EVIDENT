International Architectural Competition: The ED of the Future

We are organising an international architectural competition to design future Emergency Departments. Two parallel tracks will run: one for practicing architects and one for students. Winning entries will be prototyped in an extended reality (XR) simulation at the Swiss Centre for Design and Health and evaluated experimentally.

Study design & testing

Each winning design will be tested under two spatial conditions:

  • Intelligible layout — a layout designed to maximise visibility, clear circulation and connectivity (based on Hillier’s intelligible world & intelligible layout).
  • Unintelligible layout — a spatial configuration designed to disrupt intelligibility and test effects on interaction.

Hypotheses

We hypothesise that intelligible layouts will facilitate communication and coordination among healthcare workers, increasing the quality, frequency and spatial distribution of interactions compared to unintelligible layouts.

Measurements

  • Location tracking technologies (indoor positioning)
  • Wearable sensors (for physiological and movement data)
  • Video capture for behavioural analysis

Participants

We will recruit approximately 20 healthcare workers and 80 actors to play patients and relatives to create realistic scenarios.
